# Lab 1 <br> Introduction to CPSC250lab


Welcome to CPSC250Lab.

Most labs  in this course will cover a Python topic;
however, this first lab is intended to introduce you to the fundamental concepts
we will use to organize our work, the tools that we will use,
and the work flow that we are expected to follow throughout the course.

Because of that, this first lab has more words.
Please read them carefully, and yes you are expected to remember this for the tests!.

Keep this lab handy so you can refer to it later.

## Structure of the Lab
Labs have questions and exercises that you should complete after reading the material.  

**You are expected to complete the labs during the allotted course time!**

If you do not complete a lab, your are responsible for completing the material prior to the start of the next lab.  

If you leave early, you will only receive credit for work completed in lab!

Most of the grade for your labs this semester will come from submitting Python code to *GitLab*, a topic that will be covered later in this lab.  
Specific directions for each your labs will be given in the labs associated ```README.md``` file (**i.e., what you are reading now!**) found on the associated GitLab repo.

The README may provide web links to optional reading material.  
Unless otherwise stated, these links are for deeper study and are not required
to complete the lab.

The goal of the lab course is to provide you with an environment where you can get questions answered  as you encounter them. If the lab is easy for you and your partner, finish it and leave.  But if you do not understand what is being asked, please ask your instructor
sooner rather than later.  We don't want you to just "spin your wheels".

 Every topic builds on the previous topic, so do not assume that you will get it later.
 If you do not understand, ask during the lab period; do not delay!

This first lab is designed to allow you to explore fundamental concepts related to organization of files on your computer, and to work on your first project using GitLab.  
As such, this particular lab has more text to read and step-by-step directions to follow.  Please read carefully and follow step-by-step for this lab, even if you are familiar with the material.

For CPSC 250 lab, you should use the lab computers.  
You may use your personal machine to finish lab work after class;
however, during lecture exams you will be required to use these lab machines.  
So get used to working on them; I suggest you periodically practice on these machines before the exams.

This lab has two parts:
 1. [File Structure](docs/file_structure.md)
 2. [Git Project Repo](docs/git_project.md)


 Do each part in turn, and answer questions as you go on the provided form.  
 Your grade for this lab is a combination of the completed code and the items on the form.

The required tools are installed on the lab machines.
For setup directions for your personal machine, see [Toolchain Setup](docs/toolchain_setup.md).

## Rubric

  * 30 points - Progress/participation during lab period
  * 35 points - Part 1 complete (during class is expected)
  * 35 points - Part 2 GitLab/WebCAT complete (Look at score on WebCAT)
